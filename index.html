KNOX PARK & PERK

Make it home safe. Tomorrow's coffee's on us.

Drove downtown, then decided not to drive home? Good call. Show your bartender your booked rideshare or an activated transit ticket and get a $5 voucher for a downtown coffee shop — redeemable in the morning, when you come back for your car. Think of it as a tip, for making the responsible choice.

For Patrons

Four steps. Zero DUIs. Free coffee.

You don't have to choose between leaving your car and getting stranded downtown. Park & Perk turns the responsible choice into the rewarding one.

🅿️
1

Park & play

Drive downtown, park in a garage or lot, and enjoy your night in the Old City, Market Square, or Gay Street.

📱
2

Catch a ride

At the end of the night, book an Uber, Lyft, or cab — or hop a KAT bus or trolley — home instead of driving. Leave the car; parking in downtown's municipal garages is free overnights and weekends.

🎟️
3

Show it & grab your card

Show your rideshare screen or activated transit ticket to bar staff before you head out. They stamp and hand over a $5 Morning Perk voucher — one per ride. (Sharing an Uber? The booker gets the card.)

🌅
4

Coffee & car

Come back for your car and redeem your voucher within two days, and drive home — sober, caffeinated, and $5 richer.

The Perk

The Morning Perk Card

You've been tipping your bartender all night. This is downtown tipping you back — a $5 thank-you for making the safe, responsible call. A serialized, coaster-sized card handed out at last call and redeemed at sunrise. Simple for bartenders, simple for baristas — and it saves lives.

  • $5 value at any participating downtown coffee shop — drinks, pastries, whatever gets you moving.
  • One per booked ride, stamped with the date and the issuing bar's mark. One ride, one card — no double-dipping.
  • Valid two days from issue, so it's for your morning-after trip — not a coupon stockpile.
  • Serialized & tracked so the program can log redemptions and measure impact.

Why It Matters

The cheapest DUI prevention is a good reason to leave the car.

The biggest reason people drive home after drinking downtown is the car itself — nobody wants to deal with retrieving it tomorrow. Park & Perk flips that: your car becomes the reason you come back downtown and spend a morning here.

$5turns "ugh, my car" into "free coffee downtown"
~$10k+typical all-in cost of a first DUI in Tennessee — versus a $10 ride home
2 visitsevery voucher brings a customer downtown twice: night and morning
0the number of good outcomes from driving after last call

Program impact metrics (vouchers issued, redemption rates, late-night incident trends) will be published after the pilot in partnership with the City of Knoxville and KPD.

FAQ

What counts as "showing my ride"?

A confirmed or just-booked rideshare (Uber/Lyft), a taxi receipt, an activated Knoxville Area Transit (KAT) bus or trolley ticket, or a designated-driver pickup at the bartender's discretion. The point is simple: you're not driving.

What about my car overnight?

Good news: parking in the City of Knoxville's municipal garages downtown is free on evenings and weekends, so leaving your car overnight won't cost you a thing. Just grab it in the morning when you come back for your coffee. (Always check the posted signage for the garage you're in.)

Can I get more than one voucher?

It's one voucher per booked ride — the ride is the ticket. Vouchers are date-stamped, bar-marked, and serialized, and expire two days after issue. If your whole group piles into one Uber, that's one card (whoever booked it gets the coffee — choose your friends wisely).

What if I didn't drive downtown?

The program is designed for people whose car is parked downtown — that's the moment the risky decision happens. But bartenders have discretion; the goal is safe rides home, not paperwork.

Who pays for the coffee?

The funding model is being designed right now with our partners, drawing on sponsorships (rideshare companies, insurers, hospitals, parking operators), partner venues, and a "Perk It Forward" option that lets tonight's patrons chip in a dollar toward tomorrow's tips. What's already fixed: the perk is always free for the person who took the safe ride home.

Is this a City of Knoxville program?

Knox Park & Perk is a proposed public-private partnership, currently in the pilot design phase, seeking partnership with the City of Knoxville, the Knoxville Police Department, and downtown businesses.
