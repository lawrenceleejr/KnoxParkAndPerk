For patrons

Four steps. Two brews. Safer roads.

You don’t have to choose between leaving your car and getting stranded downtown. Pick-Me-Up turns the responsible choice into the rewarding one.

1

Park

Park in a municipal garage downtown.

2

Catch a ride

At the end of the night, book an Uber, Lyft, or cab — or hop a KAT bus — home instead of driving. Leave the car; parking in downtown’s municipal garages is free overnights and weekends.

3

Show it & grab your card

Show your rideshare screen or activated transit ticket to bar staff before you head out. They stamp and hand over a Morning Pick-Me-Up card — one per ride. (Sharing a Lyft? The booker gets the card.)

4

Coffee & car

Ride KAT back downtown free on the card, claim your free large coffee within a day, grab your car, and drive home — sober and caffeinated.

Why it matters

Safer roads start with an easy reason to leave the car.

Impaired driving hurts real people on Knoxville’s roads every year. The single biggest reason someone drives home after drinking downtown is the car itself — nobody wants the hassle of coming back for it. Pick-Me-Up removes that reason: leave the car, get home safe, and let your car be the reason you’re back downtown in the morning.

Questions

Frequently asked

What counts as “showing my ride”?

A confirmed or just-booked rideshare (Uber/Lyft), a taxi receipt, an activated Knoxville Area Transit (KAT) bus ticket, or a designated-driver pickup at the bartender’s discretion. The point is simple: you’re not driving.

What about my car overnight?

Good news: parking in the City of Knoxville’s municipal garages downtown is free on evenings and weekends, so leaving your car overnight won’t cost you a thing. Just grab it in the morning when you come back for your coffee. (Always check the posted signage for the garage you’re in.)

Can I get more than one card?

It’s one card per booked ride — the ride is the ticket. Cards are date-stamped, bar-marked, and serialized, and are valid for one day from issue. If your whole group piles into one Uber, that’s one card (whoever booked it gets the coffee — choose your friends wisely).

The card gets me free transit too?

Yes — we call it “Hair of the KAT.” While your Pick-Me-Up card is valid, show it to board any Knoxville Area Transit (KAT) bus and ride free, including your morning trip back downtown to pick up your car. One card, coffee and the ride to go get it.

What if I didn’t drive downtown?

The program is designed for people whose car is parked downtown — that’s the moment the risky decision happens. But bartenders have discretion; the goal is safe rides home, not paperwork.

Who pays for the coffee?

The funding model is being designed right now with our partners, drawing on sponsorships (rideshare companies, insurers, hospitals, parking operators), partner venues, and a “Pick It Forward” option that lets tonight’s patrons chip in a dollar toward tomorrow’s tips. What’s already fixed: the perk is always free for the person who took the safe ride home.

Is this a City of Knoxville program?

Knox Pick-Me-Up is a proposed public-private partnership, currently in the pilot design phase, seeking partnership with the City of Knoxville, the Knoxville Police Department, and downtown businesses.
