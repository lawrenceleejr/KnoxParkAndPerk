# Knox Pick-Me-Up — Proposed Logos & Custom Visual Elements

Design exploration, **not yet adopted**. The current identity (the
cup-and-steering-wheel mark) is unchanged; these are candidate directions and
supporting elements custom to this program, for review. All files are in
[`design/proposals/`](proposals) as SVG with any type converted to outlines.
Everything regenerates from `tools/` + this exploration's builder script.

---

## Logo concepts

### 1. First Light — *the sunrise in your cup*
`proposals/concept-1-first-light.svg` · `proposals/concept-1-lockup.svg`

A monoline cup with the sun coming up out of the coffee itself. The rim of the
cup is the horizon — the morning after, in one image. Geometric, one stroke
weight, reproduces at any size, works as a stamp die-line, embroiders cleanly.
The strongest candidate if the program ever wants a simpler mark than the
current cup-and-steering-wheel: it keeps the cup, keeps the sunrise, and
carries the "tomorrow" promise rather than the "don't drive" warning.

### 2. Last Call / First Light — *the bartender's stamp*
`proposals/concept-2-stamp.svg`

A seal built around the program's actual ritual: the bartender stamps your
card. A star (last call), a dotted route home, and a cup with the sun rising
in it, ringed by "KNOX PICK-ME-UP · LAST CALL · FIRST LIGHT." Designed to be
manufactured as the physical rubber stamp bars use on cards — the brand asset
and the fraud control become the same object.

### 3. Steam Road — *the way home rises off the mug*
`proposals/concept-3-steam-road.svg`

The steam off a mug drawn as a dashed two-lane road, winding up toward a
rising sun. The program's whole loop — the drive you didn't take tonight and
the one you will take tomorrow — in a single wisp. Best used large (posters,
garage banners) where the lane dashes read clearly.

### 4. Night & Morning — *the token*
`proposals/concept-4-night-morning.svg`

A circular token split at the horizon: starry night above, sunrise and a
waiting cup below. Reads at coin scale — suited to app icon, social avatar,
enamel pin, or a punched chip that KAT drivers and baristas recognize at a
glance. The most "civic seal" of the four.

## Custom visual elements

`proposals/elements.svg` — one sheet, all pieces:

| Element | Use |
|---|---|
| **Horizon divider** | Section break: a hairline interrupted by a small rising sun. A quiet brand signature between page sections and on print. |
| **Route rule** | A dotted route from a star (last call) to a cup (morning coffee) — as a divider, a receipt footer, or a progress motif across the four steps. |
| **Icon set** | Six monoline icons on a shared grid (Park, KAT, Ride, Card, Coffee, Morning) for the site's steps, signage, and wayfinding — one stroke weight, ink + one orange accent, consistent with the marks above. |
| **Coffee-ring stain** | An imperfect double ring in Sunrise Ink for coasters, posters, and envelope interiors — the one deliberately loose element in an otherwise precise system. |
| **Sunrise scallop** | A repeating half-sun border alternating Sunrise and Night along a rule — card edges, table tents, lanyards. |
| **Serial treatment** | Every card is numbered; make the number a visible feature — dashed keyline box, `Nº KPU-….` — so the fraud control doubles as a design detail. |

---

## If adopted

Any concept can be promoted into the live identity by regenerating the
collateral (`tools/build_collateral.py`) with the chosen mark and updating
`BRAND.md`. The elements sheet items are independent — the dividers, icons,
and serial treatment work with the *current* mark today and could ship without
any logo change.
